End the EU-Israel Association Agreement

The EU-Israel Association Agreement, signed in 1995 and in force since 2000, establishes strong economic, political, and social ties between the European Union and Israel. While framed as a partnership, the agreement grants Israel preferential trade access to the EU’s single market—its most valuable trading partner.

Key Provisions of the Agreement

  • Preferential Trade: Israeli goods receive tariff-free or reduced-tariff access to EU markets.
  • Political Dialogue: Allows high-level engagement on foreign policy, regional security, and diplomatic cooperation.
  • Scientific & Cultural Cooperation: Facilitates Israeli participation in EU programs such as Horizon Europe.
  • Joint Committees: Manage the implementation of the agreement, including trade disputes and economic coordination.

Why It Must Be Suspended

  • Violation of International Law: Israel continues expanding illegal settlements in the occupied Palestinian territories, in direct breach of international norms and multiple UN resolutions.
  • Human Rights Clause Ignored: The agreement includes a clause making human rights a condition for cooperation. Israel’s actions violate this, yet the agreement remains in place.
  • Economic Support of Occupation: Trade with Israel often includes goods and services originating from settlements built on occupied land.

Examples of Use

  • Settlement Products Labeled but Not Banned: Despite a 2015 European Court of Justice ruling requiring labeling of settlement goods, these products can still enter the EU market under the agreement.
  • Tariff Benefits: Israeli exports to the EU surpassed €16 billion in 2023, much of it under preferential terms.
  • Scientific Collaboration: Israeli institutions, some of which are linked to the military and surveillance industry, benefit from EU-funded programs.

Our Demands

We call on the European Union to immediately suspend the EU-Israel Association Agreement until Israel fully complies with international law, including an end to settlement expansion and human rights abuses in the occupied territories.

EU law must not be selectively enforced. Human rights should be the foundation of all partnerships—not an optional clause.
